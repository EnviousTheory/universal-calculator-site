Why Calculate SLA Outage Costs?

Service Level Agreements (SLAs) are promises of uptime. When a system fails to meet that promised availability (downtime), the financial repercussions extend far beyond simple lost revenue. Calculating these costs is essential for **risk management** and informed resource allocation.

The **UnverisalCalculator** uses a core formula to quantify this risk: Total Outage Cost = **Downtime** × **Affected Users** × **Average Revenue Loss per User/Hour**. This provides a direct, measurable value that can be used in incident reports and budget requests.

Key Factors of Downtime Cost:

  • **Direct Financial Loss:** Revenue lost during the time the service is unavailable.
  • **User Impact:** The number of users (customers or internal staff) directly affected by the service interruption.
  • **Compliance & Penalty Fees:** Costs incurred due to failing to meet contractual uptime obligations defined in the SLA.
  • **Recovery Costs:** Wages and resources spent on incident response and problem remediation.

Common Questions on Downtime & SLA

What is the difference between 99.9% and 99.99% uptime?

This difference is dramatic. 99.9% uptime (three nines) allows for about 43 minutes of unplanned annual downtime, while 99.99% (four nines) reduces that downtime to only **5 minutes and 15 seconds** per year. The additional "nine" drastically reduces operational risk.

How do I determine "Average Revenue Loss per User per Hour?"

This is often the hardest metric. For e-commerce, it's total annual revenue divided by total annual users divided by business hours. For B2B, it often reflects a portion of the client's service fee that is lost or compensated during an outage. Use a conservative estimate if you are unsure.
